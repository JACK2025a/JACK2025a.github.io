Massive Crocodile Rescued from 60-Foot Deep Well

By [Your Name], Staff Reporter December 28, 2025

GUJARAT, India — Following heavy monsoon rains, a team of specialized rescuers from Wildlife SOS successfully saved a massive Mugger crocodile that had become trapped at the bottom of a 60-foot deep well in a local village. The dangerous operation was carried out to ensure both the safety of the animal and the local residents.
+4

The rescue began after villagers discovered the crocodile struggling in the deep water at the bottom of the well. Due to the 12 feet of murky water and the narrow space, the Wildlife SOS team had to lower a rescuer and a specialized trap cage into the darkness. "Sending men down the well to retrieve it is a perilous effort, especially when you can't see below the water," noted one of the lead rescuers on the scene. After several hours of careful teamwork, the team managed to secure the crocodile and lift it to the surface using heavy-duty ropes.
+4

The result of the rescue was a success, as a veterinary check-up confirmed the crocodile was in good health and uninjured despite the fall. The animal was later transported and released into a safe, natural water body far from human settlements. This rescue is considered vital because it prevents human-animal conflict and protects a species that is an essential part of the local ecosystem.
